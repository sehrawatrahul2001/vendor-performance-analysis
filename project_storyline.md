# Project Storyline

## Problem

Large vendor portfolios often hide weak margin conversion, concentration risk, and inventory drag behind topline scale. The business can see who is big, but not always who is truly valuable.

## Approach

Rahul Sehrawat used Python to consolidate procurement, sales, pricing, and invoice data into curated summary tables, then applied SQL and dashboard logic to evaluate supplier profitability, cost efficiency, working-capital exposure, and operational reliability.

## Key Insights

- Spend and revenue are concentrated in a small supplier group.
- Inventory risk is large enough to affect cash efficiency and procurement discipline.
- Some vendors create volume without creating healthy adjusted profit.
- The best-performing suppliers combine scale with stronger margin quality and better operating characteristics.

## Business Impact

This project turns vendor reporting into portfolio management. It supports negotiation, supplier prioritization, purchasing discipline, and cost optimization while showing how Rahul Sehrawat brings real operational context into analytics work.
