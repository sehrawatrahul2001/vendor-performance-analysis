# Business Problem Report

## Project Title

**Vendor Performance & Cost Optimization Analysis**

## Prepared By

**Rahul Sehrawat**

## Business Context

In procurement-led and FMCG-style businesses, vendor performance cannot be judged on spend or revenue alone. Suppliers also affect freight burden, sell-through quality, stock build-up, and working-capital efficiency. When these signals are reviewed separately, supplier risk stays hidden inside large purchase volumes.

## Problem Statement

The business needs a structured way to determine:

- which suppliers are strategically valuable
- where spend concentration is becoming risky
- which vendors are weakening profit conversion
- where inventory is building faster than sales
- which relationships require negotiation, tighter control, or rationalization

Without that integrated view, vendor decisions stay reactive and margin leakage continues unchecked.

## Analytical Objective

This project was designed to:

1. consolidate raw purchase, sales, pricing, and invoice data into curated vendor assets
2. measure vendor economics across revenue, cost, freight, profit, and stock exposure
3. identify top performers, weak performers, and inventory-heavy suppliers
4. package the findings into a management-ready supplier review

## Stakeholder Value

This analysis is relevant for procurement leaders, finance teams, operations managers, and analysts who need a stronger basis for supplier planning. It also reflects Rahul Sehrawat's operational background by framing analytics around follow-through, capital efficiency, and commercial decision quality.
